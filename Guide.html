The Machine Stops compared to 1984


Similarities Differences
1984 The Machine Stops
1. Both dystopian texts People are controlled through fear People treat the Machine as a god and do not say anything bad about it
2. People rarely meet up in person Winston rarely talks to anyone Vashti talks to people through the Machine
3. Barely any freedom People are watched by the Party 24/7 through telescreens People obey the Machine's traditions
4. Lots of technology Telescreens to spread propoganda and to watch peole The Machine keeps everyone alive
5. One person disobeys the rules Winston has a relationship that he was not allowed to have Kuno goes on the surface through an air vent, not the usual exit
6. End with the protaganist being defeated Winston gives in and obeys the Party Kuno and everyone else is killed when the Machine breaks
7. Misinformation The Party change the past Basically all information is passed down from lectures and no one collects any information on their own


The Machine Stops compared to modern day life

The Machine Stops Modern day life
1. People communicate through video calls on a round plate People communicate through video calls on phones
2. People can stop incoming calls by "isolating" themselves People can put phones on Do not disturb mode
3. People use the Machine for everything People use phones, iPads, laptops and more for everyday life
4. People do not question information There is fake information online but most information is reliable and people check info
5. People rarely leave the rooms People can stay indoors if they want to because of food delivery, internet calls etc.
6. People do not know how to survive without the Machine People use a lot of technology which they do not understand but it is possible to live without technology
7. People rarely meet in person People frequently meet in person, often in groups
