Other Books

One of the main books that influenced the writing of E.M. Forster's The Machine Stops was A Modern Utopia by HG Wells. In this book, 2 travellers are teleported to a utopian future. This utopia is controlled by one government who ensure that peace is kept. Rules are enforced by Samurai. Drugs and alcohol are banned. People enjoy a lot of personal time but they are required to take an annual week-long ramble in the wilderness. Men and women are equal. All poverty and inequality is gone. Everyone has access to education and healthcare. Machines are improving daily to increase the productivity of work. Wells compares the utopia to 20th century life. He explains that no world can be perfect, but we can keep improving it.
